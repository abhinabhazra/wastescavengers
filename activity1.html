Activity 1

Waste Scavenger Hunt

 

Procedure

To try and answer the followong pressing and urgent questions about waste: What is waste? Why is it such a problem? Is all waste the same? How much waste do we produce? How amI contributing to the waste problem? How are we managing our waste? Can waste be a resource? How can our waste management practices be more sustainable?

 
 
 
 

Waste Scavenger Hunt Worksheet

The activity started off by assigning each student a classroom which they were to examine. After seeking permission for the teachers, each students thoroughly examined each designated room, taking careful note of the location and material of the waste. Each student made observations and notes based on the type of waste, volume of the waste and where the waste was kept. Key locations such as the staffroom, canteen and school kitchen were also covered. The students were able to find that the type of waste varied between different locations and that areas such as staffroom and school kitchen were cleaner that the classrooms. Classrooms that had younger students had more variety of waste as compared to classes of older students. This activity gave the students insight on the different types of waste. It was also observed that more waste was produced at school than in individual households, where the students were able to come the conclusion that as there are more people in schools, more waste gets produced.

 

Classroom IX-B

In classroom IX-B, the waste items comprised food wrappers, a Mazaa bottle, paper, and food scraps, all of which were placed inside the bin and likely disposed of by the students.

Classroom VIII-B

In classroom VIII-B, the waste materials consisted of paper, plastic wrappers, a pen, a bottle cap, candy wrappers, and pencil shavings, some of which were found both inside the trash can and scattered on the floor. It is probable that both the teachers and students disposed off this waste.

 

Classroom VIII-A

In classroom VIII-A, the waste consisted of tissue paper, foil, paper, fruit peels, plastic, and pencil shavings, the majority of which were properly disposed of in the bin, presumably by the students.

Classroom IX-D

In the classroom IX-D, the waste materials included wrappers, tissue paper, and a banana peel, all of which were placed inside the trash can. The students disposed of it.

 

Classroom VI-D

In classroom VI-D, the waste comprised paper foil, candy wrappers, dust, and a partially consumed apple. This waste was found both inside the bin located under the desks and in close proximity to the bin. It is likely that both the teachers and students disposed it.

Classroom IX-C

In classroom IX-C, there was a variety of waste materials, including candy wrappers, a food wrapper, a pen, a partially eaten apple, used paper, a plastic bottle, paper plates, and foil. These items were found both inside the trash bin and in the vicinity of the bin and students' desks. The disposal of this waste involved both students and teachers.

 

Canteen

In the school canteen, there was a variety of waste materials, such as tissue paper, food scraps, straws, paper straws, plastic wrappers, paper plates, coupons, spoons, forks, and sauce packets. A significant portion of this waste was not inside the designated trash bin; instead, it was left on the counter. The responsibility for disposing of this waste fell to the canteen workers.

Classroom IX-A

In classroom IX-A, a diverse array of waste materials was present, comprising plastic wrappers, corn, a pen, paper, and a banana peel. Most of these items were properly placed inside the trash bin, with the exception of the paper. Both students and teachers were involved in the disposal of this waste.

 

School Kitchen

In the school kitchen, a diverse range of waste materials was present, including cucumber peels, foil, corn, lemon, seasoning packets, plastic packets, coconut shavings, a bag of chips, and a milk packet. Everything, with the exception of the cucumber peel, was appropriately placed inside the bin. The kitchen staff disposed of the waste.

Biology Laboratory

In the biology laboratory, the waste consisted of onion peels, disposable cups, a cloth bag, a plastic sheet, and dried leaves. All of these items were correctly deposited in the trash bin. The disposal of this waste was managed by the teachers.

 

Staffroom

In the staffroom, there was a mixture of waste materials, including banana peels, a plastic bag, pencil shavings, and a candy wrapper. Two of these items were found inside the bin, while the remaining two were not. The teachers were responsible for the disposal of this waste.

What are natural resources?


Natural resources are materials or substances that exist in the environment and are valuable to humans for various purposes. Some examples of natural resources include water, air, sunlight, soil, minerals, forests, wildlife, and fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas. These resources play a crucial role in supporting life and various human activities, such as agriculture, energy production, and manufacturing. Proper management and conservation of natural resources are essential for sustainable development and the well-being of the plane

However, an object that is broken cannot be used efficiently in that state. The decision of whether or not something becomes waste is that of the owner of the product.
