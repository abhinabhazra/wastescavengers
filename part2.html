The Waste Segregation Game

 

Procedure

In this waste categorization activity, participants follow a step-by-step process. First, they label six containers representing different waste categories. Then, the class is divided into two teams. Each team selects 10 items from their school's waste scavenger hunt sheet and writes the names of these items on separate cards. These cards are combined into a common bowl, resulting in a total of 20 cards. Team members take turns drawing a card and placing it in the correct waste container. For every correct placement, a point is earned. The game continues until all cards are used, and the team with the most points at the end wins, promoting awareness of waste management and recycling.

 
 
 
 

The Waste Segregation Game

In the waste separation game, there were four categories waste, wet waste, biodegradable waste, and constructive waste. Instead of using the waste cards we used actual waste material. We conducted the activity in one of the classrooms where the children were shown the waste and were asked to segregate it into one of the categories. The children enjoyed the activity and it was an educational experience.
